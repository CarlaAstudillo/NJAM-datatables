New Jersey Diocese Credibly Accused

The list includes all priests and deacons that the dioceses determined was “credibly accused” of sexual abuse of a minor. There is no strict definition of credibly accused, but it usually means the accusation was deemed by church officials to have some merit and was not believed to be a blatantly false accusation.

The list includes only priests and deacons assigned to the five New Jersey dioceses. It may not include priests who belong to specific Catholic orders, which operate under a different leadership than the local dioceses. So, Jesuit priests, Benedictine monks, Franciscan priests and others serving under a religious order at a Catholic school or other institution in New Jersey may not be included.
